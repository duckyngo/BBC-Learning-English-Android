<p><strong>Phrasal verbs with direct objects</strong></p>
<p>The noun object can usually go before or after the adverb.</p>
<p><em>I <strong>broke off</strong> our engagement.</em></p>
<p><strong><em>OR</em></strong></p>
<p><em>I <strong>broke </strong>our engagement <strong>off</strong>.</em></p>
<p>But if we use a direct object pronoun, remember it can only go between the verb and the adverb.</p>
<p><em>WRONG: I broke off <strong>it</strong>.<br>RIGHT: I broke <strong>it</strong> off.</em></p>
